Chapter Twelve: The Escape

Dear Diary,

She was at it again today. I told her I didn't want to go. But as usual my pleas fell on deaf ears. I watched her as she meticulously gathered some items and set them on the kitchen table. One by one. She moved swiftly for a frail old lady. Or at least that's what she wants people to think.

Song playing in the background "If I could turn back time."

But she doesn't fool me. Those foster kids across the street don't trust her either.

"Don't open the door!"

I can hear her bones creaking as she starts to walk towards me. She's louder than the cries from the beaten wooden floor under that old rocking chair of hers.

Does she seriously think I'm not going to fight.

"I think she's dead"

Suddenly the creaking stopped. What is she doing? Oh. She fell asleep. Why do they say 'fall asleep'. She didn't fall. She is just standing there, dreaming away.
